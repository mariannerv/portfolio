About the project

This project was developed as our final degree project and is by far the biggest and most complex project I've worked on. The premise of the project was to develop a Lost and Found System with an auction component. Essentially, if a person found a lost object, they would take it to a registered police station, where information about the object would be registered. This information would include a description, a picture, the location and time it was found, its brand, category, and other relevant details.

If someone lost an object, they could register it online, the same way one would register a found object. The person could then crosscheck their lost object with all the found objects in the system and if there was a match they and the police station would be notified.

During the first semester, from September 2023 to December 2023, we planned out the entire project. We researched the technologies and tools we would use, decided on the methodology (SCRUM in this case), created a Gantt chart for our tasks, assigned everyone to specific areas of the project, and developed an RMMM plan, which proved to be extremely useful.

In our second semester, from January 2024 to June 2024, we executed the plan as best as we could. There were plenty of challenges along the way: some people gave up, some struggled more than others, and all of us put in far more work than we anticipated. But in the end, it all worked out.
